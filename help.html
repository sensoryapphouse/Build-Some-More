Build Some More

A cause-and-effect activity program for children and young people with learning difficulties

Getting Started

The main screen shows two rows of picture buttons:

  • Top row — Activity categories (Animals, Babies, Patterns, Counting, Television, etc.)
  • Bottom row — Individual activities within the selected category

Use the arrow buttons on either side of each row to scroll through more categories or activities. Tap or click on a category to select it, then tap an activity to start.

Playing an Activity

Each activity shows a picture that is built up piece by piece. Press the switch (tap/click the screen, press a key, or use an external switch) to reveal the next part of the picture. Sound effects play with each press.

When all parts have been revealed, a completion reward animation plays with a golden glow, and then the next picture in the activity loads automatically.

Press the back arrow (top-left corner) or the Escape key to return to the selection screen.

Switch Modes

Tap the settings cog (bottom-right of the selection screen) to choose a switch mode. Four modes are available:

Mode Indicators Description
Single Switch Any press advances the picture. Ideal for users with one switch or simple touch access.
Alternating Users must alternate between switch 1 (left/red) and switch 2 (right/blue). The active switch is shown brightly; the other is dimmed.
Random The required switch is chosen randomly each turn. The user must press the correct one.
Cooperative + Both switches must be held down at the same time. Designed for two users working together.

Switch indicators appear at the left and right edges of the screen during activities. A brightly lit indicator means that switch needs to be pressed; a dimmed indicator means it is already held down (cooperative mode).

Input Methods

Touch / Mouse

  • Tap or click anywhere on the activity picture to activate
  • In two-switch modes, tap the left half of the screen for switch 1 (red) and the right half for switch 2 (blue)

Keyboard

KeyAction
Space or 1Switch 1 (left / red)
Enter or 2Switch 2 (right / blue)
EscapeReturn to selection screen

Gamepad / External Switches

The program supports standard gamepads and USB/Bluetooth switch interfaces that appear as gamepad buttons. Button 1 maps to switch 1, and button 2 maps to switch 2.

Activity Categories

The program includes a wide variety of activities across many categories:

  • Animals — Farm animals, young animals, wildlife
  • Babies — Baby-themed cause-and-effect pictures
  • Blackboard — Drawings revealed on a blackboard background
  • Burst — Animals, clowns, people, and objects that burst onto screen
  • Clowns — Colourful clown characters built step by step
  • Counting — Number activities with rockets, cars, saxophones, and more
  • Eyes — Fun eye and face activities
  • Hippy — Colourful hippy van designs
  • Painting — Paint-by-numbers style activities
  • Patterns — Geometric patterns, spirals, and designs (multiple sets)
  • Scenes — Complete scene pictures
  • Shapes — Flowers, stars, and other shapes
  • Space — Rockets, robots, and space-themed pictures
  • Television — Pictures revealed inside a TV frame
  • What Is It — Mystery pictures gradually revealed
  • All Sorts — A mixed collection of various activities

Each category contains multiple activities, and each activity contains several pictures that cycle automatically.

Background Music

Some activities include background music that plays while the picture is being built. The music fades out when the activity ends or when you return to the selection screen.

Tips

  • The program runs in landscape orientation on iPad and iPhone
  • For the best experience, use the app in full screen
  • Switch mode settings are remembered between sessions
  • In cooperative mode, two people can each press one side of the screen simultaneously
  • External switch interfaces that emulate keyboard keys (Space/Enter) or gamepad buttons will work with all switch modes
